### Execution plan

#### Phase 1: Listening & profiling (Week 1–2)

- Spend 1 hour/day reading threads on all platforms
- Build a spreadsheet of recurring complaint types:
  - "Management company ignoring repair requests"
  - "Unexplained service charge increase"
  - "Section 20 notice — what do I do?"
  - "Managing agent not providing accounts"
  - "How do I escalate to First-tier Tribunal?"
- Note which questions get most engagement and no good answers
- Identify 10–15 power users / advocates who give good advice regularly (potential partners/beta users)

#### Phase 2: Establish presence (Week 3–6)

**Rule: Give value first, every time. Never lead with product.**

For each platform, create a dedicated account (or use existing if Svetlana has one):
- Reddit: new account, age it by commenting helpfully for 2 weeks before posting
- Facebook: personal account or page, depending on community rules
- MSE/LandlordZone: register, fill out profile, post a few helpful replies first

**Weekly cadence:**
- Mon/Wed/Fri: scan all communities for new posts matching target pain points
- Reply to 2–3 threads per day with genuinely useful answers
- Response template structure:
  1. Validate the frustration ("This is extremely common, you're not alone")
  2. Explain what's actually happening in plain English
  3. Give concrete next steps (cite legislation where relevant: LTA 1985, s.20, RTM, etc.)
  4. End with soft mention: *"We're building a tool specifically for this — happy to share if useful"*

**Content to prepare upfront (reference library):**
- Plain-English explainers for top 20 leasehold situations
- Key legislation summaries (LTA 1985, Commonhold & Leasehold Reform Act 2002, Building Safety Act 2022)
- Template letters (service charge challenge, repair demand, complaint escalation)
- First-tier Tribunal process walkthrough

#### Phase 3: Proactive posting (Month 2+)

Once trust is established, post original content:
- "I helped 5 leaseholders dispute service charges this month — here's what worked"
- "Section 20 notice arrived? Here's exactly what to do in the first 7 days"
- "Your managing agent has 14 days to respond to this. Do they know that?"

On Reddit: post as text posts with real substance (Reddit hates thin content).
On Facebook groups: longer posts with screenshots/examples where allowed.

**Soft CTA options (rotate):**
- "We're building Leasehold Case Copilot for exactly this — waitlist at [URL]"
- "I built a tool that does this automatically — DM me if you want early access"
- "We're looking for 5 buildings to test this with for free"

#### Phase 4: Community feedback loop (Ongoing)

- Every conversation is product research
- Log recurring questions → feed into SEO content plan
- Identify power users who could become ambassadors
- Invite engaged users to beta test → collect testimonials

### Production workflow (per article)

1. **Research:** Read top 5 ranking pages. Note what's missing or badly explained.
2. **Outline:** Structure around the user's actual question, not SEO boilerplate.
3. **Write:** Plain English. No jargon without explanation. Concrete steps.
4. **Legislation check:** Every claim linked to actual law or official source.
5. **CTA:** End with relevant CTA (waitlist, free tool, template download).
6. **Publish:** Blog on product site (or Ghost/Substack if site not ready).
7. **Distribute:** Share in relevant Reddit threads ("wrote this guide that might help"), LinkedIn, email list.
8. **Update:** Revisit every 6 months as legislation changes.
